The current global population and interconnectedness have generated a large amount of data, which requires analytics. To run a successful business, it is necessary to make sense of the data provided by customers and market competitors.

In today's competitive market, data and analytics provide a competitive advantage to businesses. This includes improving their ability to make fact-based decisions and develop data-driven products and services. As a result, analytics, data collection and analysis, is critical and should be at the heart of every business.

Today, companies are gravitating toward data analytics, or web analytics, a variant of it, in order to make sense of and leverage data. Consequently, data analytics has become the foundation of every new and established business. The extraction and categorization of web data in order to interpret patterns, trends, and optimize web usage is known as web analytics.

1. Creating Actionable Insights

It isn't enough to simply have insights from data; businesses must also act on them, because insights are meaningless without actions. This is one of the major analytics trends of 2022, with its emphasis on acting on actionable insights garnered from data.

Data analytics has traditionally been read-only. Insights are created, but their application is left to the organizations or businesses. However, this is changing, with technological innovations like artificial intelligence and machine learning allowing businesses to take action with business intelligence tools.

2. Using External Data

The quantity and quality of data are both important. The more analytics data used by businesses to make actionable insights, the better. This has prompted businesses, including those that had previously relied on their own data, to supplement their data sets with external data.

3. Combine Data for Efficient Operations

The value of using external data in addition to internal data to generate actionable insights cannot be overstated. This has prompted companies to gather data from different areas, platforms, and channels. However, while this is important, the data needs to be standardized and centralized.

4. Consumer-personalized Data

It is important to have actionable insights that reflect customer behavior and are able to adapt to customers' and market dynamics. The understanding of customers' behaviors, needs, and preferences through data analytics enables businesses to create approaches that will effectively meet their target market.

5. Using Gathered Data Responsibly

Before personal data can be collected, customers' trust must be gained. And once trust is established, it should not be abused. This brings us to an important rule: data privacy. The increasing abuse of data and scandals have made the public wary about sharing personal data with the public.

6. Data Cloud Migration

One of the prevalent data analytics trends today is the migration of data from on-site to the cloud. The benefits attached to data cloud migration are significant. As a result, businesses are moving their data and analytics from in-house to the cloud.

7. Context-enriched Analytics

It is estimated that by 2025, context-driven data analytics, alongside artificial intelligence, will replace more than half of traditional data models. Context-enriched analytics is built on graph technology, helping businesses to have deep data analytics.

8. Democratization of Data

The need and benefits of data analytics are now being seen in the operation of businesses. Today, companies are democratizing and creating data-driven environments that will enable their employees with no core data background to become data analysts.

9. Testing Strategies

With the ability of customers to be targeted across different platforms, businesses must be willing to test strategies created from actionable insights before their full launch. This approach allows businesses to analyze content and strategies from different channels.

10. Businesses Need to Know Their Target Customers

Different models are needed to understand customers and properly empathize with them. To provide a great customer experience, businesses need to understand and empathize with their customers by studying their behaviors, needs, preferences, and motivations.

Final Words

Data analytics is no longer an afterthought in today's highly competitive market, but an intentional and necessary activity needed to make actionable insights. The highlighted trends in customer web analytics are essential for running a successful business and improving customers' experience.

The first ever email was sent in 1971, meaning it has been around for five decades. It begs the question of its relevance today, especially in light of the prevalence of social media and other marketing platforms.

While it is human nature to get caught up in the excitement of new inventions while forgetting about the old, this is not the case with email marketing. It has endured as a highly effective marketing tool.

What is Email Marketing

Email marketing is a marketing strategy that aims to raise brand or business awareness or boost sales by communicating with people via email. The marketing tool is used to promote businesses' products or services. In addition, it can also be used as a means of keeping in touch with target customers.

This marketing strategy is cost-effective and has a high conversion rate. These email marketing statistics will show how invaluable this marketing tool is.

Email Marketing Statistics (General)

According to Statista (2022), the number of email users in the world is estimated to reach 4,258 million in 2022. In addition, the estimated number of emails sent and received is expected to reach 333.2 billion in 2022. With its wide reach and users, email marketing remains a strong force in marketing today.

The importance of email marketing today cannot be overemphasized. Campaign Monitor reported that email marketing is used by 64% of small-scale businesses to reach their customers. Furthermore, Litmus found that 41.5% of brands saw email marketing as critical to their success.

According to OptinMonster, 99% of users with email accounts open their emails every day. In addition, 37% of businesses are willing to improve their email marketing budget to reach this high audience in 2022.

According to Databox (2022), 33% of marketers send emails once a week, while 26% send them several times per month. Revenue generated from email marketing is projected to reach $9.62 billion by 2022.

Business-to-Consumer (B2C) Email Marketing Statistics

Business-to-consumer email is a direct interaction between businesses and their end users, the customers. According to SaleCycle, 59% of customers believed marketing emails to be instrumental in their decision to purchase from a brand. Furthermore, 61% of customers favor email as the means of communication from businesses.

Email newsletters are believed to be the most effective marketing channel to nurture leads. According to the Content Marketing Institute, 16% of B2C marketers agreed with this.

Business-to-Business (B2B) Email Marketing Statistics

Email marketing isn't limited to interaction between businesses and their customers alone, but also between one business and another. According to WordStream (2022), 59% of B2B marketers perceive email marketing as the best means to generate revenue. In addition, 77% of B2B marketers make use of this marketing strategy to improve sales and traffic to their website.

According to the Content Marketing Institute, email engagement is the top metric used by B2B marketers to measure campaign performance. Email newsletters are considered by 31% of B2B marketers as the best way to create market leads.

Automation Email Marketing Statistics

Email automation is used in email marketing to nurture leads, for conversion, and to retain customers. According to Emailmonday (2022), 51% of companies currently make use of email automation. Email automation generates 320% more revenue than non-automated campaigns.

According to Content Marketing (2020), 85% of marketers make use of automated campaigns. The three major benefits of email automation are: 30% saving of time, 22% lead generation, and 17% increase in revenue.

Final Words

Email marketing is one of the oldest marketing tools, yet remains one of the most effective today. The statistics behind email marketing show that it is still widely adopted by brands and businesses. The wide reach of email today means companies are willing to improve their budget to fully optimize it.

The high rate at which people open their email and their preference for it as a means for interaction shows why businesses will keep using it as a marketing tool. If you haven't thought about creating awareness for your brand or increasing sales with email marketing, now is the time.

Social media has been around for years, used by billions of people in their everyday lives to reach and connect with friends, families and colleagues. It started off as a way to connect with people, but currently connects brands and businesses to far and wide audiences. The traditional face-to-face or posters marketing is fast giving way for this wide-reach marketing tool, making it the best marketing tool.

This article is for beginners in social media marketing. You are off to a great start with these powerful tips and guides.

What is Social Media Marketing

Social media marketing provides one of the most important aspects of a business—marketing, but on a larger and wider scale. The social media platforms for marketing include: Twitter, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn, etc.

Benefits of Using Social Media as a Marketing Tool

The benefits of social media marketing today cannot be overemphasized, with large corporations and small-scale businesses alike leveraging its powerful reach.

Some of the core benefits of social media marketing include:

  • Awareness creation
  • Increased sales
  • Income generative
  • Community building
  • High efficiency

Best Social Media Marketing Tips and Guides for Beginners

Social media marketing is one of the easiest and most efficient forms of marketing today, all thanks to the internet. Marketing on social media is for everybody. With these beginners' tips, you can enjoy its benefits in your business and brand.

1. Identify and Understand Your Audience

You need to ask questions like, "who is my audience?" Understanding who your product or business is for will be the foundation of your social media marketing. Once you understand the demography, either Gen Z, millennial or older, activities they enjoy, and everything about them, then your marketing strategy will be efficient.

2. Highlight Ways to Reach Your Audience and Choose the Best Social Media Platform

Understanding your audience will give you a great start in identifying the best social platform to reach them with. If they are young, love looking good and post how beautiful they are, then the obvious social media platforms to market your beauty products are Instagram or TikTok. Identifying different ways, and choosing the best that will reach your audience is a great strategy.

3. Have Clear Goals

You can get overwhelmed when you have different goals. Some marketing goals include engagements, awareness, high traffic, community building, etc. Setting aside specific marketing goals and following them means you won't get distracted.

4. Choose Between Organic or Paid Growth

This is another important marketing strategy. Organic growth is the best for small businesses and also cheap, but your reach might be limited. Conversely, you can make use of paid ad campaigns or social media influencers on popular social media platforms. This growth is fast, but more expensive. So, depending on your business or brand, you might desire organic growth or choose to pay for ads to reach high audiences faster.
